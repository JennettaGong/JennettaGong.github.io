abstract: >-
  The development of Autonomous Vehicle (AV) has created a novel job, the safety driver, recruited
  from experienced drivers to supervise and operate AV in numerous driving missions. Safety drivers
  usually work with non-perfect AV in high-risk real-world traffic environments for road testing
  tasks. However, this group of workers is under-explored in the HCI community. To fill this gap, we
  conducted semi-structured interviews with 26 safety drivers. Our results present how safety drivers
  cope with defective algorithms and shape and calibrate their perceptions while working with AV. We
  found that, as front-line workers, safety drivers are forced to take risks accumulated from the AV
  industry upstream and are also confronting restricted self-development in working for AV
  development. We contribute the first empirical evidence of the lived experience of safety drivers,
  the first passengers in the development of AV, and also the grassroots workers for AV, which can
  shed light on future human-AI interaction research.
